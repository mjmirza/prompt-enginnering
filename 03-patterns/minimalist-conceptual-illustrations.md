# Prompt Templates for Minimalist Conceptual Illustrations with Infographic Elements

This document provides prompt templates to consistently generate images in the "Minimalist Conceptual Illustration with Infographic Elements (Hand-Drawn / Sketch Aesthetic)" style. This style effectively conveys complex ideas and emotions through a blend of conceptual metaphor, minimalism, infographic clarity, and a human-touched hand-drawn aesthetic.

---

## Understanding the Style: Key Characteristics

* **Conceptual Metaphor:** Abstract ideas represented by concrete, relatable objects/scenarios.
* **Minimalism:** Lack of clutter, clean white/light backgrounds, essential elements, limited color palettes.
* **Infographic Elements:** Simple diagrams, labels, arrows, visual flow to explain relationships, processes, and components.
* **Hand-Drawn / Sketch Aesthetic:** Slightly imperfect lines, visible strokes, natural texture for a human, non-futuristic feel.
* **Emotional & Subjective Layer:** Chosen metaphors evoke feelings and personal connection.

---

## Your Prompt Template Set

Here are 5 versatile prompt templates. Just fill in the bracketed `[PLACEHOLDERS]` with your specific ideas.

### Template 1: Representing a Core Concept / Metaphor

* **Use when:** You have a central, strong metaphor for an idea (e.g., decision, knowledge, potential, growth).

    ```
    Conceptual illustration, hand-drawn sketch aesthetic, minimalist. A [NOUN representing core concept, e.g., 'weighing scale', 'open book', 'key', 'seedling'] at the center. It visually demonstrates [ABSTRACT IDEA, e.g., 'balance and decision', 'hidden knowledge', 'unlocking potential', 'growth from effort']. Subtle infographic arrows/lines show [BRIEF EXPLANATION OF FLOW OR CONNECTION]. White background, clean lines, limited earthy color palette.
    ```

* **Example Fill-in:**
    `Conceptual illustration, hand-drawn sketch aesthetic, minimalist. A **weighing scale** at the center. It visually demonstrates **balance and decision**. Subtle infographic arrows/lines show **the process of evaluation**. White background, clean lines, limited earthy color palette.`

---

### Template 2: Showing a Process / Transformation

* **Use when:** You want to show how something changes, evolves, or is simplified.

    ```
    Minimalist conceptual illustration, hand-drawn style. A [STARTING STATE, e.g., 'tangled knot', 'scattered puzzle pieces', 'complex diagram'] transforming into a [ENDING STATE, e.g., 'clear path', 'interlocked system', 'simplified icon']. Infographic elements clearly illustrate [KEY STAGES OR STEPS]. White background, focus on flow and clarity, muted colors.
    ```

* **Example Fill-in:**
    `Minimalist conceptual illustration, hand-drawn style. A **tangled knot** transforming into a **clear, unravelled thread**. Infographic elements clearly illustrate **the steps of problem-solving**. White background, focus on flow and clarity, muted colors.`

---

### Template 3: Highlighting a Challenge / Barrier

* **Use when:** You want to illustrate an obstacle, risk, or something being restrained.

    ```
    Conceptual illustration, hand-drawn, minimalist. A valuable [OBJECT, e.g., 'glowing gem', 'stack of insights', 'delicate idea'] is being [ACTION, e.g., 'protected', 'held back', 'contained'] by a [BARRIER, e.g., 'cracked glass dome', 'heavy chain', 'veiled curtain']. Small infographic icons represent [TYPES OF CHALLENGES, e.g., 'vulnerability', 'hesitation', 'complexity']. White background, emphasis on tension and fragility, subtle color accents.
    ```

* **Example Fill-in:**
    `Conceptual illustration, hand-drawn, minimalist. A valuable **glowing gem** is being **protected** by a **cracked glass dome**. Small infographic icons represent **security vulnerabilities**. White background, emphasis on tension and fragility, subtle color accents.`

---

### Template 4: Emphasizing Human Interaction / Control

* **Use when:** The human role (architect, leader, reviewer) is central to the message.

    ```
    Minimalist conceptual illustration, hand-drawn sketch aesthetic. [NUMBER] human hands are [ACTION, e.g., 'gently guiding', 'carefully connecting', 'thoughtfully reviewing'] an abstract [OBJECT/CONCEPT, e.g., 'flow of ideas', 'interlocking components', 'data stream']. Infographic labels highlight [ELEMENTS OF CONTROL OR INSIGHT]. White background, clean, expressive lines, warm tones for human elements.
    ```

* **Example Fill-in:**
    `Minimalist conceptual illustration, hand-drawn sketch aesthetic. **Two** human hands are **carefully connecting** an abstract **flow of ideas**. Infographic labels highlight **key decision points**. White background, clean, expressive lines, warm tones for human elements.`

---

### Template 5: Combining Text & Visual Metaphor (for specific message)

* **Use when:** You have a specific short phrase that needs direct visual reinforcement.

    ```
    Conceptual illustration, hand-drawn, minimalist. The main text "[YOUR CORE PHRASE HERE]" is at the top. Below, a central [VISUAL METAPHOR, e.g., 'stylized master key', 'thought bubble with a question mark', 'tree with branching paths']. Infographic elements within or around the metaphor detail [KEY SUB-POINTS, e.g., 'prompting style, tool, implementation', 'options A, B, C', 'root cause, effects']. White background, clean, bold typography for main text, subtle infographic styling.
    ```

* **Example Fill-in:**
    `Conceptual illustration, hand-drawn, minimalist. The main text "**THE SECRET TO SUCCESS**" is at the top. Below, a central **stylized master key**. Infographic elements within or around the metaphor detail **strategy, execution, iteration**. White background, clean, bold typography for main text, subtle infographic styling.`

## Key Tips for Using These Templates:

* **Be Specific in Placeholders:** The more concrete your `[PLACEHOLDERS]` are, the better the result.
* **Maintain Aesthetic Keywords:** Always keep terms like "conceptual illustration," "hand-drawn sketch aesthetic," "minimalist," "white background," and "infographic elements" to guide the style.
* **Think Metaphorically:** Before picking a template, think about what simple object or scenario best represents your abstract idea.
* **Color Control:** If you want specific colors, add them explicitly (e.g., "red thread," "golden key," "muted blue tones").
